What is "The Network"?

The Network is the name given to the collective wealth comprised by Mudd's student run servers. It may or may not be, strictly speaking, legal, but because of the closed nature of Mudd's intranet, it is protected from outside scrutiny.

This means that once something is on the Network, it is safe to grab for yourself, whatever it might be (disclaimer: this is a general rule. Rule #2: don't be dumb).

What is M2M?

Short answer:
Your lord and savior, the gateway to paradise.

Long answer:
M2M is a search engine, which indexes the files hosted by the servers on the Network. You can search for whatever you want, and if it's publically available, and older than about 23 hours, M2M will show you where it is. Sometimes you can directly download it from the search results page, you lucky late-comers you.

What is a server?

Okay, so, I know they say there's no such thing as a dumb question, but if you're at this school, and you need to ask this question, then...something might have gone wrong with the admission process. I'm just saying. In any event, I'm sure there is someone within 20 feet of you who is not only able but willing to wax poetic about what it means to be a 'server' on the Network.

Briefly, a server is any computer that is configured to share things on the Mudd network. At least, that's what I will mean by 'server' anytime you see it on this site, unless specifically redifined elsewhere.

Can I run a server?

No. A server runs you. Or maybe that's just M2M.

Seriously, though; see the server FAQ for more about setting up and running your own server.

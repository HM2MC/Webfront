Who runs servers?

Short Answer:

At HMC, select students.

Long Answer:

You do! You, being the students, as distinct from the school itself (which has its own servers, but with which M2M does not, at this time, truck). And probably not you, since you're looking at this FAQ. But, hey, you never know.

Anybody can run a server -- your neighbors might, your friends might -- if you're not careful, even YOU might be running a server right now.

But who, specifically, runs servers? Well, I run M2M (obviously). But a complete list of all the people who run servers, and which servers they run, is a bit beyond the scope of this site. Not to mention my interest.

How do I run a server?

Short Answer:

Blood sacrifice, and a liberal sprinkling of baby's tears

Long Answer:

On purpose?

It's a simple-yet-difficult process, running a server. Here's what you'll need, at the very least:

  • (1) computer, with keyboard
  • (1) ethernet cord
  • (1) email address
  • the ability to read (nice; one down already, amirite)
  • the ability to type, or a friend who you trust and can type for you
These are your tools. Now, for the meat:
  1. Choose a name for your server. This is the most important goddamn step, because you are saddled with this fucking name forever, unless you want to be a dick and make CIS do more work than they ought to. They get enough flak (sometimes deservedly) and do not need extra shit to do. Rule #1.
  2. Get your ass to the Static IP Request Form, and fill it out. Your reason for the request should be something like "local ssh access"; they don't, in my experience, ask too many questions, and will complete your order within a week. You'll get an email confirmation when it's done, with the details.
  3. Follow CIS's instructions about configuring your network settings. If you don't know how to do this, check out this resource for all the info you need.
  4. Get SAMBA sharing enabled; windows has this automatically, I think, since it's their protocol, but macs and unix will have to do some (not much) voodoo. I would recommend against installing Samba4 just yet, because it doesn't work properly.
  5. Set up your samba shares, a topic which you can find plenty of information on yourself.
  6. Put content in your samba shares
Annnnd that's all there is to it.

What is direct serving?

Short Answer:

Letting people download through M2M.

Long Answer:

If you love your downloadees, you can configure your server to allow M2M to access them directly, instead of forcing people to use their browsers for SAMBA linking. This isn't much of an improvement for those people searching on a Mac or Unix box - those OS's handle samba links sanely.

It is a huge improvement for Windows users, who previously had to navigate to files manually in their explorers. Let me tell you, that is a pain.

Direct serving allows everybody to download through their browsers, rather than samba, using HTTP requests.

How do I set up direct serving?

Short Answer:

Depends on your server-set up.

Long Answer:

  1. Install your webserver of choice, if you don't already have one (I recommend Apache or NGINX).
  2. Set up a normal host, but change it so that it listens on port 13213 instead of 80.
  3. Configure it so that it treats every file as an application, rather than something a browser knows how to serve.
  4. create a directory with symlinks to your samba share directories, and set that as the root of your host.
  5. Let me know that you've set up direct serving.
